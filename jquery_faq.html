Yellowstone

  • Yellowstone encompasses 3,472 square miles (2,221,766 acres) which makes it larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined.
  • Yellowstone is home to more than 500 active geysers.
  • Yellowstone is home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states (67 species).
  • There are approximately 1,000 miles of hiking trails in Yellowstone.

Sequoia

  • The park was established in 1890 to protect groves of big trees, or giant sequoias.
  • The largest big tree in the park is known as the General Sherman tree and is thought to be 2,300 to 2,700 years old.
  • Sequoia National Park is one of the eight national parks in California.
  • Sequoia National Park has about 1.3 million visitors per year.

Yosemite

  • The park’s diverse landscape supports more than 400 species.
  • Yosemite is home to one of the tallest waterfalls in the world which stands at 2,425 feet.
  • Yosemite is one of the few places in the U.S. where you can see a rainbow at night.
  • Yosemite is the only national park to bid to host the winter Olympics.
